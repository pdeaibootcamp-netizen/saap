The product · one paragraph

A monthly sector brief for Czech SME owners — plain-language, verdict-first, delivered by their bank.

Strategy Radar is a web app for owners of 10–100 employee Czech firms. Once a month they receive a short written briefing — two to three pages — about what is happening in their sector and how their business compares to similar Czech firms. The briefing ends with 2–4 concrete actions, each labelled with a time horizon. It is delivered as an email, a page inside the application, and a downloadable PDF.

Primary persona

The Exposed Owner — sole strategic decision-maker, triggered by a pain event, evaluates fast and skeptically, must see proof of insight within 14 days.

Primary channel

ČS relationship managers introducing the product to existing SME clients inside George Business. Direct sign-up is secondary.

What's free, what's free-ish

Free during validation. Monetisation deliberately deferred — v1 validates engagement, data acquisition, and lead flow first.

What Arkana does not replace is the human call — the orchestrator and user still picked which hypotheses were load-bearing, which risks to take, and which scope to ship.

Validated pains

Three hypotheses carry the product

From the ranked hypothesis framework (H1–H13), three rose to the top with the most severity and the most cohesive story. All three are unaddressed by any incumbent serving SME operators.

H3
"I have no idea whether my numbers are good, bad, or average."
Inability to validate whether internal financial performance represents success or failure.
Highest severity
H2
"I negotiate prices from fear, not from market knowledge."
Pricing conversations without external reference cost owners real money.
High severity
H1
"Big strategic calls are made on unvalidated assumptions."
Absence of peer operational benchmarks leaves the owner flying blind on hiring, pricing, capex.
High severity
H13
"Market shifts land in my P&L before I see them coming."
Reactive firefighting instead of strategic response — because market context is missing.
Moderate severity

Competitive gap: 0 of 15+ incumbents (Statista, D&B, CB Insights, AlphaSense, Stripe benchmarks, …) address H3 for SME owner-operators. They all target analysts, investors, or lenders. The SME operator is structural whitespace.

The full vision

Five capability surfaces — one integrated product

Arkana's North Star is structurally explicit: five surfaces, each with MVP → advanced → north-star maturity progression per feature. One surface is the MVP anchor; the rest accrue over subsequent increments.

01

Sector Briefing Engine

Monthly 2–3 page brief calibrated to the owner's sector, closing with 2–4 time-horizon-tagged actions. MVP anchor.

02

Peer Position Engine

Percentile position against segmented cohort (NACE × size × region). Embedded in briefs at MVP; standalone later.

03

Shift Detection & Alerts

Plain-language alerts when the owner's relative position drifts; cause attribution distinguishes owner-driven from cohort-wide.

04

Pre-Loaded Benchmark Report

Day-one insight at first login — bank-data pre-processing or manual input fast-track. Post-MVP.

05

Configurable Dashboard

Owner-prioritised view across four metric categories. Dashboard work begins in v0.2 PoC (Part II). Post-MVP in full.

Staged delivery

MVP + four increments + the North Star

Theme arc: Static → Continuous → Personalised → Predictive → Autonomous. Feature count expands roughly 4×: from 7 at MVP to 40+ at the North Star, all within the same five-surface frame.

MVP

Now

First brief. Static, human-authored, 7 features.

Inc 2

Continuous monitoring. Alerts, cohort stats, history.

Inc 3

Sector intelligence depth. Evidence, cause attribution, data pre-processing.

Inc 4

Deep personalisation. LLM-generated narratives, multi-metric correlations.

Inc 5

Predictive maturity. Forward-look projection, multi-year view, EN support.

North Star

Real-time recalculation. Full causal decomposition. Predictive percentile projection.

MVP is deliberately thin: the brief alone, the minimum-viable cohort math, and the three delivery surfaces. No automation. No alerts. No dashboards. Just enough to answer "would an owner open it?"

The compression

Traditional discovery is 4–12 weeks. Arkana-style discovery is 1–3 days.

The AI shift isn't "the AI decides what to build". It's "the AI collapses the cost of producing every intermediate artefact so the human can focus on the judgement calls".

What the AI does

  • Persona generation from structured problem briefs
  • Competitor landscape scanning + pattern extraction
  • Trend synthesis from public sources
  • Solution scoring against ranked hypotheses
  • Roadmap decomposition with maturity tagging

What the human does

  • Picks which hypotheses to bet the product on
  • Decides MVP scope vs. vision scope
  • Owns every product principle ("verdicts, not datasets")
  • Makes every privacy & trust call
  • Signs off on what ships to real customers

What gets better

  • Breadth — more competitors, more personas
  • Rigor — typed artefacts, traceable deltas
  • Consistency — one voice across all documents
  • Iteration speed — re-run with new inputs in minutes
  • Document discipline — living specs, not lost decks

The working setup

One orchestrator, four specialists, each with a lane

The orchestrator decomposes the user's request, delegates in parallel when work is independent, merges outputs, surfaces conflicts to the human. Specialists never edit outside their lane — a pre-commit hook enforces this.

The discipline that makes async work

One owner per directory — enforced, not encouraged

Every doc has exactly one owner. Specialists cannot edit outside their lane — a pre-commit hook rejects the write. Conflicts surface at the orchestrator layer where a human can see them.

Directory Owner What lives there
docs/product/ Product Manager Feature PRDs, user stories, PDRs, glossary, assumption log
docs/design/ Designer Flows, screen states, component specs, accessibility checklists, copy
docs/engineering/ Engineer Architecture notes, ADRs, tech decisions, test plans
docs/data/ Data Engineer Data model, pipelines, cohort math, privacy & compliance
docs/project/ Orchestrator Plan, decision log, open questions, backlog, changelog, this deck
src/ Engineer All application code & tests

Every decision is either in decision-log.md (logged, dated, cited) or in open-questions.md (unresolved, owned). Nothing lives only in a transient conversation.

v0.1 — what actually happened

From scope-lock to running code in five phases

Every phase closed with a gate: orchestrator reads all outputs, reconciles conflicts, logs cross-cutting decisions, the user ratifies, the next phase unblocks.

Phase 0 — Scope Lock 2026-04-17 7 open questions → 7 decisions + 2 backlog items. User-decided.
Phase 1 — Foundation 2026-04-17 8 artefacts in parallel: privacy architecture, cohort math, 2 ADRs, metric list, assumption log, IA, trust patterns.
Phase 2 — Feature specs 2026-04-20 9 feature PRDs + 9 designs across 3 tracks. Frozen Czech vocabulary, frozen time-horizons.
Phase 3 — Implementation 2026-04-20 Next.js 14 + Supabase + RLS. Analyst authoring, consent, onboarding, owner brief, revocation, publish pipeline.
Phase 4 — Verification 2026-04-20 V1–V8 checks: action count bounds, validity floor, no-raw-number rule, privacy separation, delivery fidelity.

The branch trial-phases-2-4 is frozen at the end of Phase 4 as a reference v0.1 state.

Non-negotiables

Eight product principles — guardrails on every feature

Every feature either respects these or it gets pulled. Each specialist reads them at the top of every task. Violations are escalated, never silently accepted.

1. Briefs are the atomic unit of value

Every feature produces, enriches, or distributes a brief — or it is out of scope.

2. Verdicts, not datasets

Every output is a conclusion. No raw numbers without a comparison. Ever.

3. Plain language, no jargon

No statistical notation. Reads like a well-informed accountant speaking.

4. Proof of value before anything else

First session produces a verdict in under 60 s for bank-referred users. No config walls.

5. Privacy is a product feature

Client data never enters base-model training. Brief / user / RM / credit data stay architecturally separate.

6. Lead signals are opportunity-flavoured

Nothing ever reads to the owner as "the bank is watching me for credit risk". That collapses trust.

7. Bank-native distribution

George Business embedding is the primary channel. Direct sign-up is the backup.

8. Every interaction is data acquisition

Every touchpoint considers what data we could learn for a richer future output.

Why this product, from ČS's perspective

Three business goals, each traceable in every artefact

Every workstream maps back to at least one goal. The build plan checks this explicitly. Features that don't thread to a goal are out of scope or deferred.

🎯

Recurring engagement of SME clients with ČS, with the product as a reason to return.

📊

G2 · Data depth & cadence

More client data, in more detail, more often. The primary instrument for sustained SME data contribution.

🤝

G3 · RM lead generation

Opportunity-flavoured signals RMs can act on. Deferred past MVP (not risk-framed).

The moat — why this is defensible

20 000+ anonymised Czech SME financial records already live at ČS — structurally unreplicable. 70–90% CAC reduction vs. direct B2B SaaS through the RM channel. Trust transfer from ČS: owners share data with their bank in ways they would not with an outside vendor. All three fuel the give-to-get flywheel: each participant widens the gap.

What the PoC is for

Customer testing is the point

v0.2 exists so a moderator can put a Czech SME owner in front of this, hand them a session, and see what lands. We're testing concept — not pixel polish, not pricing.

What we want to surface

  • First-minute reaction — does it feel like their sector, or like generic content?
  • Plain-language test — any sentence a participant re-reads is a failure
  • Action concreteness — "could I actually do that?" beats "interesting idea"
  • Comparison realism — "that matches what I thought" vs. "that's surprising-but-plausible"
  • Trust reactions — does the bank-sourced analysis reassure or raise new concerns?

What we explicitly won't drill on

  • UI polish and visual design — PoC, not launch
  • Pricing / subscription — free during validation
  • English version — Czech only for now
  • Real-time alerts or dashboards — Increment 2+
  • Features not visible in this PoC
"It's a monthly briefing from Česká Spořitelna about how my business is doing compared to similar Czech firms, with a few concrete things I could do — written for me, not for an analyst." — what a participant should be able to repeat after the session.
