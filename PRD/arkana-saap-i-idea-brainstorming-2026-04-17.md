### What It Is

It is a persistent benchmark monitoring platform that continuously tracks an SME owner's key financial and operational metrics against a segmented peer cohort — and alerts them the moment their relative position shifts in a way that matters.

This is not a reporting tool that generates snapshots on demand. It is a live instrument that holds an owner's current standing inside their peer group and watches it change over time. When a metric drifts — when labor costs rise from the 34th to the 58th percentile while the cohort median holds flat — the platform surfaces that movement in plain language, with enough context to distinguish the owner's problem from a market-wide shift. It sits at the intersection of financial performance monitoring and competitive intelligence, but built entirely around the operator rather than the analyst. It is the core subscription product, priced at €4–6k annually, designed to generate the recurring revenue the business model depends on.

### What It Does

SME owners currently have no reliable way to know whether their financial performance is strong or distressed relative to peers. A 12% gross margin and a labor cost ratio of 38% are just numbers without a reference frame — they cannot tell an owner whether to hold course, cut costs, or reprice. This is the performance validation gap at the center of H3, the highest-severity confirmed problem. The platform closes that gap not by generating a one-time benchmark report but by maintaining a persistent, updated view of where the owner stands — and detecting when that standing deteriorates before the owner would otherwise notice.

The secondary leverage is on H1: strategic decisions made without peer validation. Owners currently make capital allocation, pricing, and hiring decisions without knowing whether they are operating from a position of relative strength or weakness. This platform gives them that orientation continuously, so decisions are grounded in comparative reality rather than internal intuition alone.

### How It Works

At onboarding, the owner's financial data — sourced either through bank data integration (with consent) or manual input — is mapped to a segmented peer cohort defined by industry, size band, and geography, satisfying the functional minimum established in the Segmented Benchmarking market standard. For bank-referred users, a pre-loaded benchmark report is waiting at first login, delivering immediate comparative standing before the owner has taken a single action. This is the highest-leverage time-to-value mechanism available at the GTM stage.

The dashboard then becomes the owner's persistent reference point. Four metric categories — financial performance ratios, cost structure, revenue dynamics, and operational efficiency — are tracked against the peer cohort over rolling time windows. The owner can configure which metrics occupy their primary view, satisfying the customizable dashboard requirement identified as a table-stakes launch feature. The display does not show raw numbers in isolation; it shows position — second quartile, 34th percentile, top 20% for their sector — so the owner always reads their data in comparative context rather than in a vacuum.

When a metric's percentile position moves beyond a defined threshold — either set by the owner or calibrated automatically to signal meaningful drift — the platform generates a plain-language shift alert. The alert identifies what changed, over what time window, and whether the movement reflects the owner's own trajectory or a cohort-wide shift. Exports in Excel and PDF are available at any point, meeting the data export requirement and supporting use in conversations with accountants, advisors, or bank relationship managers. All four non-negotiable table-stakes features — structured pre-processed output, customizable views, data export, and monitoring alerts — are present at launch.

### Target Segment

The primary entry point is The Exposed Owner — the beachhead buyer who arrives triggered by a specific pain event: a margin squeeze, a pricing challenge from a competitor, a cost line that has climbed without explanation. This segment evaluates quickly and skeptically, requires a concrete proof of insight within 14 days, and makes the purchase decision unilaterally within a discretionary budget. The platform's onboarding design — pre-loaded benchmark report, immediate percentile positioning, first alert within the trial window — is calibrated precisely to this segment's evaluation pattern. The Bank-Referred Passive Adopter is the volume channel, activated when ČS relationship managers introduce the platform in client conversations; for this segment, the pre-onboarded report at first login is the critical conversion mechanism, since the RM's referral creates the opening but the product must close it immediately.

### Differentiation

#### Market Opportunity

This platform directly captures the peer benchmarking opportunity identified as the single root capability that unlocks all high-priority confirmed problems simultaneously. H3, H1, and the adjacent pressure on pricing decisions (H2) all share the same root absence: no segmented, real-time peer comparison calibrated to Czech SME context. A single persistent monitoring instrument that maintains that comparison continuously captures that entire opportunity in one product surface rather than requiring separate tools for each problem.

#### Competitive Gaps Exploited

The platform exploits the most consequential gap in the competitive landscape: H3, the highest-severity problem in the confirmed list, is completely unaddressed by any existing competitor as a value proposition. Statista offers industry-level statistics; CB Insights benchmarks companies for investors, not for the companies themselves; Stripe's benchmarking covers payment and commerce metrics only. None of them tell a Czech manufacturer or professional services firm whether their EBITDA margin, overhead ratio, or revenue-per-employee signals strength or distress relative to direct peers. The structural blind spot — every competitor is built for the analyst, not the operator — means there is no credible incumbent to displace. The monitoring and alerting capability, identified as a competitive expectation from the synthesis, is present but has never been pointed at the SME operator's relative peer position. That specific application is unoccupied.

#### Defensible Advantage

The moat is the dataset, not the dashboard. The platform's benchmark quality is a direct function of the anonymized financial and operational data contributed by the 20,000+ ČS business clients in the underlying pool — a dataset no external competitor can purchase, scrape, or replicate. Each new participating SME improves cohort depth, sharpens segmentation granularity, and increases the accuracy of shift detection. This is the give-to-get data network effect identified in the competitive synthesis: the product becomes more valuable as participation grows, which attracts more participants, which further widens the gap between this dataset and anything a new entrant could assemble. The trust transfer from the ČS bank brand reduces the data contribution barrier — owners are far more willing to share financial data with their primary bank than with an unknown third-party vendor — which accelerates the network effect precisely at the cold-start stage where it is most fragile. Privacy-preserving benchmarking architecture ensures that no individual firm's data is ever exposed, which is the technical precondition for the model to function at all. Over time, the compounding depth of a proprietary, continuously updated SME dataset becomes structurally unreplicable — not because competitors lack the technology, but because they cannot access the raw material.
