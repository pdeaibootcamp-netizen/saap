- [P2-S1] Owner/CFO purchases or downloads sector overviews from Statista or Euromonitor to anchor market size, growth, and macro factors (statista.com, euromonitor.com). Friction: Global framing, limited Czech SME granularity; high cost-to-relevance ratio.
- [P2-S2] CFO/finance manager pulls company/credit reports on key customers/competitors from CRIF/CRIBIS and D&B (cribis.cz; dnb.com/cs-cz). Friction: Risk-oriented footprints rather than operational benchmarks; no sector/size-adjusted performance medians.

Friction: Narrative lacks credible peer comparisons; stakeholders ask for “how do we compare?”
- [P4-S3] If uncertainty persists, they commission a limited-scope consultant review or ask a trusted advisor for a quick take. Friction: Advice perceived as generic; low implementation usefulness; slow feedback loop.

### Phase 5: Execution and team alignment
- [P5-S1] Owner/CFO selects a path (e.g., reprice contracts in next cycle; renegotiate supplier terms; pause expansion). Friction: Decision confidence remains low without benchmarks.
- [P5-S2] CFO/commercial director turns decision into targets and actions in OKR tools (Cascade/Quantive/WorkBoard) or spreadsheets; assigns owners and due dates. Friction: Manual re-entry from Excel; no link back to external signals.
- [P5-S3] Board/owner review: CFO presents deck with updated scenarios, expected P&L impact, and any external citations. Friction: Repeated challenge on assumptions; iterative edits before sign-off.

### Phase 6: Ongoing monitoring and review
- [P6-S1] Monthly review: finance refreshes Excel dashboards; checks bank balances in George Business; validates FX/energy trends via ČNB and news sources (cnb.cz; hn.cz/e15.cz). Friction: Reactive monitoring; no alerting by relevance to firm specifics.
- [P6-S2] Ad hoc: Owner repeats ChatGPT/Copilot queries; scans LinkedIn/industry forums; occasionally buys another report when a new question arises. Friction: Fragmented toolchain; no persistent memory of past decisions or outcomes.

## Primary Problems

- **H2:** Pricing negotiations conducted from position of fear rather than market knowledge — Problem Score 18. This is a universal business pain affecting most B2B SMEs; negotiations happen constantly, the emotional distress is severe ("I dread," documented anxiety), and quantified revenue losses are substantial (€27K-340K per instance across multiple personas). Market structure ensures anyone without pricing power faces this; evidence spans manufacturing, services, consulting. The combination of high recurrence, severe intensity, and broad population makes this a top-tier business case.

- **H3:** Inability to validate whether internal financial performance represents success or failure — Problem Score 19. This is the highest-scoring problem because it's catastrophic chronic uncertainty affecting daily operations. Business owners cannot answer "are we doing well?" — an identity-level question for entrepreneurs. The pain is continuous (daily for some), affects diverse personas (owners and CFOs across sectors), and the emotional language ("terrifies me," "dread") indicates this is existential rather than operational. Structural to information-poor SME environments.

- **H1:** Strategic decisions made on unvalidated assumptions due to absence of peer operational benchmarks — Problem Score 14. This drives severe misallocation of capital (€2.1M investments, €180K foregone profit) at quarterly/annual planning cycles. Evidence shows this affects both owners and CFOs across sectors, suggesting it's endemic to Czech SME strategic planning. While less frequent than daily pricing or performance anxiety, the magnitude of consequences and broad applicability justify primary investment.

- **H4:** Competitive intelligence gathering consumes disproportionate time while producing minimal actionable insight — Problem Score 13. This is a massive productivity drain (120-180 hours quarterly) happening continuously. 6 of 12 personas across all three types reported this; the pattern is structural to competitive analysis in opaque markets. While individual pain is moderate (frustration), the frequency is extreme, the population broad, and the cumulative time waste represents significant opportunity cost across the market.

## Secondary Problems

- **H5:** Major capital allocation decisions made without ability to validate ROI assumptions against peer outcomes — Problem Score 11. Severe when it occurs (€900K lost savings, €150K sunk costs) but episodic (major capex decisions are infrequent). 5 of 12 personas across industries. Limited from primary by frequency; not all SMEs face constant investment decisions. However, for growth-stage businesses or capital-intensive sectors, this is a critical pain point worth addressing as a tier-two priority.

- **H12:** Post-decision regret and inability to learn from experience due to lack of outcome validation — Problem Score 12. This is chronic emotional burden (persistent regret, inability to improve) affecting 5 of 12 personas continuously. Limited from primary by population frequency; requires analytical sophistication and self-awareness that not all SME owners have. However, for those affected, this compounds over time and prevents organizational learning, making it strategically important even if not universally felt.

- **H11:** Chronic anxiety and sleep disruption from operating without external reference points — Problem Score 13. Catastrophic for those affected (2-3 nights/week sleep disruption, physical symptoms) but affects a narrower population (4 of 12 personas). Not all business owners internalize uncertainty as anxiety; some have higher risk tolerance or better coping. However, for high-stress owners, this is a severe quality-of-life issue that could drive premium willingness to pay.

- **H6:** Board/investor presentations undermined by inability to provide external validation of strategic assumptions — Problem Score 10. Real financial consequences (€8K/year higher rates, credibility loss) at quarterly governance touchpoints. Limited from primary by population frequency; specific to businesses with formal boards or external financing. Most micro-SMEs don't face this, but for PE-backed or scaling businesses, this is acutely felt and affects capital access.

- **H19:** Founder/owner credibility erosion when unable to answer basic market position questions from stakeholders — Problem Score 9. Severe emotional impact ("humiliated," "exposed") but episodic (during financing or governance events). 4 of 12 personas. Limited by frequency and population; not all SMEs engage with sophisticated stakeholders regularly. However, for those scaling or seeking external capital, this is a painful barrier that could justify targeted solutions.
